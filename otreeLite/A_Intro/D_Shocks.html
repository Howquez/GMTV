There is a chance of {{ risk }}% that a shock occurs. Such a shock may reduce your endowment to up to {{ damage }}%. While you do not have any influence on the occurence of a shock, you can affect the damage it evokes.

The more you and the rest of your group contribute to the group account, the smaller the damage if a shock occurs. Conversely, the lower the contributions, the higher the damage if a shock occurs.

More precisely, there won't be any damage if everybody in the group contributes his or her whole endowment to the group account – even if there was a shock. If none of you contributes anything to the group account, a shock will cause a damage of {{ damage }}% of the endowments of each and every one of you.

Note that we provide a calculator behind the -symbol. It takes the abovementioned formula und helps you to consider different cases. As such, it is most important that you understand that higher contributions lead to lower damage and vice versa.

Show formula

$${{damage}}\% \times \bigg(1-\frac{\text{Sum of contributions} }{ \text{Sum of endowments} } \bigg)$$
